Social Media vs Social Networking

Today, we are going to talk about an everyday habit. Social Media! Social media has multiple websites and apps that help keep in contact with others’ loved ones. Social media also creates businesses and interacts with the user’s audience. Another way of communicating is social networking. Social Networking focuses on forming and nourishing the connection between two or more people. Social networking also focuses on maintaining the connection. This difference is significant because both concepts have similar functionalities. The difference is what the user uses the platform for. For more information, you can reference WaywardKind.

When networking, we can present ourselves or a different persona. We want people to get to know us from our perspectives. When posting updates, it’s crucial to practice social media safety. Social media safety focuses on bringing a safe, positive environment. Here are some social media safety tips to follow. Simple tips to follow are making your accounts private, not publishing personal information, not publishing where you are traveling/located, not using social media on public Wi-Fi, and more. (UAB) Significant tips to practice are learning how to report, block, or filter media shown, watch out for suspicious messages, look before you click a post, and do privacy check-ups on your settings. (RAINN) For more information on receiving help, look into the Organization of Social Media Safety.
